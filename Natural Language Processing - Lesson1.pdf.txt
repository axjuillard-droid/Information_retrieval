Definition (according to wikipedia)
Natural language processing (NLP) is the processing of natural 
language information by a computer. NLP is a subfield of computer 
science and is closely associated with artificial intelligence. NLP is 
also related to information retrieval, knowledge representation, 
computational linguistics, and linguistics more broadly.[1]
Major processing tasks in an NLP system include: speech 
recognition, text classification, natural language understanding, 
and natural language generation.

Some definitions
• N-gram = subsenquence of n items
• The items can be phonemes, syllables, letters, words
• Corpus (plural corpora) = a computer-readable collection of text 
or speech
• Lemma = a set of lexical forms having the same stem, the same 
major part-of-speech, and the same word sense
• Types = number of distinct words in a corpus
• Vocabulary = set of all distinct tokens of a corpus
• Tokens = (not always) the words
• Bag of words = set of words (without sequence order)
• Lemmatization = reducing a word to the root
Example : am, are and is have the shared lemma be
Stemming = cutting suffixe of words
Example :
– ATIONAL → ATE (relational → relate)
– ING → _ (doing → do)
– SSES → SS (grasses → grass)
Stemming and Lemmatization are useful to reduce vocabulary entropy

Subword Tokenization : Byte-Pair Encoding
• Byte-Pair Encoding(2016)
• To define a vocabulary : learn how to split words according to a 
corpus
Byte-Pair Encoding(BPE)
• reduces out-of-vocabulary issues,
• balances vocabulary size and contextual expressiveness,
• preserves frequent whole words while breaking rare words into 
meaningful parts. Control the exact vocabulary size
• BPE Tokenizer is used by Open AI in GPT, GPT-2, but also in several 
other transformer based models like LlaMA, RoBERTa, BART, 
DeBERTa…

BPE algorithm
• 0) vocabulary is all possible characters {a,b,…}
• Repeat :
1) Find the most frequent adjacents tokens (Ex: ‘a’ , ‘n’)
2) Concate and Add to the vocabulary (Ex: ‘an’)
3) Replace occurrences of step 1) by concatenated one of step 2)
• Until : #k merges made

WordPiece
• Tokenizer used in BERT family models like DistilBERT, MobileBERT, 
Funnel Transformers, MPNET... 
• Similar to BPE tokenizer, but :
• [UNK] for unknown tokens
• ## prefixfor subwords that don’t start a word
• Each merge of A and B tokens are scored :
 Score(A, B) = (freq(AB) * len(vocab)) / (freq(A) * freq(B))
 priority to the merged tokens where individual parts are less 
frequent

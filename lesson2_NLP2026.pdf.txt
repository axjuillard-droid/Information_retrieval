IR Evaluation
• The accuracy of an information retrieval task is measured in terms 
of precision and recall.
• Suppose that a given IR system returns X documents when a 
query is fired. But the actual or gold set of documents that needs 
to be returned is Y .
IR Evaluation
• Recall may be defined as the fraction of gold documents that a 
system finds. It may be defined as the ratio of true positives to the 
combination of true positives and false negatives.
• Precision may be defined as the fraction of documents that an IR 
system detects and are correct.
• F-Measure may be defined as the harmonic mean of precision and 
recall.

TF-IDF
• TF-IDF = term frequency–inverse document frequency
• TF or term frequency : may be defined as the total number of 
times a given token exists in a document divided by the total 
number of tokens.

TF-IDF
• IDF or inverse document frequency : the specificity of a term 
can be quantified as an inverse function of the number of 
documents in which it occurs.
